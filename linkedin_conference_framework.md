# LinkedIn Conference Framework — John O'Dea
## AI Conference — Pre/During/Post Posting Guide

---

## OVERVIEW

This framework gives you a structured approach to LinkedIn posting around the conference — building presence before you arrive, capturing value while you're there, and socializing your learnings after. For someone starting fresh on LinkedIn, this is a low-pressure way to establish a voice and demonstrate thought leadership without it feeling forced.

**The goal:** By the end of the conference cycle, you have 4–6 posts that establish you as a thoughtful, experienced voice at the intersection of healthcare payor data and AI — and give potential connections a reason to reach out.

---

## PRE-CONFERENCE POST
### Timing: 3–5 days before the conference
### Purpose: Signal you're going, invite connections, establish your angle

**Template:**

---

*Heading to [Conference Name] next week — my first time attending and I'm going with a specific question in mind.*

*I've spent 30 years in healthcare payor data — claims, HEDIS, risk adjustment, Snowflake architecture — and over the past year I've been using AI as a genuine thought partner in that work. Snowflake Cortex, Claude — not to automate analysis, but to go deeper into data faster than was ever practical before.*

*The question I'm bringing to the conference:*

*How do you move from personal AI productivity to organizational AI intelligence? It's one thing to make one analyst faster and more insightful. It's another to make the whole organization consistently smarter — where AI draws from governed definitions everyone agrees on, and the answers are the same quality regardless of who's asking or which analyst they reached.*

*That's the problem I'm trying to solve. Looking forward to finding others thinking about the same thing.*

*If you're going to be there — let's connect.*

*#AI #HealthcareAnalytics #DataGovernance #SnowflakeData #HealthcarePayor*

---

**Notes:**
- Keep it personal and specific — your angle is distinct
- The question format invites engagement
- Don't oversell — curiosity reads better than credentials at this stage

## GENERAL LINKEDIN VOICE GUIDELINES

**Do:**
- Write in first person — your humanized voice translates perfectly to LinkedIn
- End posts with a question or invitation — it signals openness and invites engagement
- Be specific — name the tool, name the problem, name the domain
- Acknowledge uncertainty — "I'm still figuring this out" is more credible than "here's the answer"
- Keep paragraphs short — one to two sentences max per paragraph on LinkedIn

**Don't:**
- Open with "I'm excited to share..." — it's the most skipped opener on LinkedIn
- List everything — pick one or two things and go deep
- Over-hashtag — five to seven targeted hashtags maximum
- Wait too long post-conference — recency matters for engagement

## METRICS TO WATCH

For a fresh LinkedIn profile, don't optimize for viral reach — optimize for the right conversations:
- **Profile views** — spikes after posts signal the right people are finding you
- **Connection requests** — quality over quantity; healthcare, data, and AI folks are the target
- **Comments** — engagement beats impressions; one substantive comment is worth 100 passive views
- **DMs** — the real goal; posts that generate direct outreach are working
